Dataset for Biomechanics

Aggregated data from all public AddBiomechanics uploads to enable large-scale machine learning

This is the world's largest dataset of physically validated human motion. We provide skeletons, their physical properties such as link masses and inertias, all the joint positions, velocities, and accelerations over time, along with all the measured contact forces with the ground and all joint torques computed with inverse dynamics.

We regenerate our aggregate dataset every day at approximately midnight. The datasets come in efficient on-disk binary formats that support reading individual frames off of disk in random orders during training, reducing the amount of RAM needed to use these datasets for ML training. Example code to use the data for training a simple PyTorch model is provided along with the data.

The "Physics Capture" Challenge:

Estimating Accurate Physical Motion from Noisy Motion Capture

We have a lot of ways to get noisy motion capture signals for the human body (pose estimation from video, wearable IMU sensor suits, optical motion capture, etc) but it currently requires expensive force measurement equipment to accurately reconstruct the physical aspects of motion, including the forces between the feet and the ground, as well as joint torques. This challenge aims to bridge the gap, going from noisy motion capture to clean and accurate physical human motion dynamics.
